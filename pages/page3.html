Scott Eaton's Artist+AI
Figures & Form in the Age of Intelligent Machines

A relatively young medium of art is explored as Scott Eaton combines an artist's wit and a trained AI's computations.

The artist

Scott Eaton is a London-based artist whose art is mostly based on the human body and its anatomy in the form of drawings, digital sculptures, photographs, and AI-assisted art. He earned a master's degree from MIT Media Lab and worked as an animator for well-established companies such as Pixar, Disney, Sony, Microsoft, and Warner Bros. Recently, he changed his focus towards machine learning as a "creative collaborator" with his artistic process.

The process

The particular AI tool that Scott Eaton used is called a convolutional neural network (CNN). A neural network is composed of thousands and up to millions of nodes that do simple math but when formed into a network, can do complex tasks. It essentially tries to mimic the human brain’s network of neurons. CNN is just a specialized form of neural network for dealing with images efficiently and effectively.

Over the years, Scott has been training his CNN which he calls “Bodies neural network”. He did this by feeding it a well-lit picture of the naked human body he shot in his studio and a drawing of the picture in his style. Then, the “Bodies network” learns the correspondence between a human figure and his drawing style. Thus, this allows him to draw a stylized and almost abstract figure and use the “Bodies network” to infer the human anatomy and paint it.
